My current side project, and work in progress over the past 8 months or so. College Wiz uses free, public data provided by IPEDS to filter colleges across the US.

My partner and I felt that a lot of college search sites were old and outdated. We wanted to spice things up and make the user experience a little more intuitive. We decided to build this public facing page so we could get more familiar with the IPEDS dataset. The real project is just getting started ;)

Remember PokemonGo? If not, you must have been on a remote island during the summer of 2016. You probably could've caught some pretty nice Pokemon there too.

During the PokemonGo frenzy, a couple co-workers and I wanted to cheat the system a bit. We wanted to crack the spawning algorithm and figure out where the hell we could catch a dragonite. To do so, we did a 24 hour hack-a-thon, creating this PokemonGo sightings page. A simple right click allowed you to add a sighting to the map, and a click on an existing Pokemon allowed you to verify the sighting. We even had a 'live' mode, only showing Pokemon sighted in the last 15 minutes. At its peak, we were getting over 1000 visits per day, with a total of over 2000 Pokemon locations entered.

Unfortunately for our map, we soon learned that Pokemon spawns were fairly random, enough to render our map useless. However, in our short time, we did get to solve some pretty cool problems: Spam protection, so people can't draw male genitalia out of Abras in the Atlantic ocean (yes, that happened), clustering, and creating the sleekest interface of all the PokemonGo maps out there!

For the time being, this site is not being hosted anywhere. I may work on getting it back online, but if you'd like to see it, it'll have to be in person.

This 3D cube racer game is one of the most addicting indie games I've ever played. This was done as a 3 week project in my senior year at RIT. I got a lot of positive feedback and decided to work on it a bit after the project ended - adding swipe controls for mobile, adjusting the scoring, and making it more playable. Ultimately it wasn't as fun on the mobile browser and there's no way to port the 3D code to a mobile platform, so here it lays, consuming hours of my life everytime I click on the link.
